# Organization Boilerplates

The boilerplate is the standard "About [Organization]" paragraph that appears at the end of every press release. It should be 2-3 sentences covering mission, key facts, and website.

**Replace the placeholder below with your organization's actual boilerplate.**

---

## How to Write a Good Boilerplate

A boilerplate should answer three questions in 50-75 words:
1. **What does the organization do?** (Mission, not history)
2. **Why does it matter?** (Scale, impact, or distinction)
3. **Where can someone learn more?** (Website URL)

**Weak boilerplate:**
> "Founded in 1987, Acme University is a leading institution of higher education committed to excellence in teaching, research, and service."

**Strong boilerplate:**
> "Acme University is a research university serving 18,000 students across 12 colleges, with particular strength in journalism, public health, and environmental science. Its faculty publish in top peer-reviewed journals and advise policymakers at the state and federal level. Learn more at acmeuniversity.edu."

## Notes on Usage

- Update this file whenever the organization's mission, key metrics, or leadership changes
- The boilerplate should not change between releases — consistency is the point
- For joint press releases with partners, include both organizations' boilerplates, with the primary organization first
- Keep it current: an outdated boilerplate with wrong enrollment numbers or an old website is worse than no boilerplate
