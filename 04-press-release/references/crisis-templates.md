# Crisis Communication Templates

Pre-approved statement frameworks for common crisis scenarios. Fill in the bracketed fields for your organization. All statements should be reviewed by legal counsel before distribution.

---

## How to Use This File

1. Identify the crisis type below
2. Copy the relevant template
3. Fill in all bracketed fields with verified, confirmed information only
4. Remove any sections that don't apply
5. Have legal and leadership review before release

---

## Template 1: Data Privacy Incident

**Use when**: A breach, unauthorized access, or exposure of user/customer data has occurred.

---

STATEMENT FROM [ORGANIZATION NAME] REGARDING DATA SECURITY INCIDENT

[Date]

[Organization name] recently discovered that [brief description of what occurred — e.g., "unauthorized access to a portion of our customer database"]. We are writing to inform [affected group] and to explain the steps we are taking in response.

**What happened**: [1-2 sentences describing the incident in factual, non-technical terms. Include approximate timeframe.]

**Information involved**: [List specific types of data affected — e.g., names, email addresses, account numbers. Do not speculate about data that may have been accessed but is not confirmed.]

**What we are doing**: We have [list concrete, specific actions — e.g., "secured the affected systems," "engaged a leading cybersecurity firm," "notified law enforcement"]. We are also [ongoing action].

**What you can do**: [Specific steps affected individuals can take — e.g., "monitor your accounts," "reset your password," link to resource page.]

For questions, contact [Name, Title] at [email] or [phone number]. [If applicable: We have established a dedicated hotline at [number], available [hours].]

We take this matter seriously and will provide updates as our investigation progresses. Our next update will be provided by [specific date].

[Name, Title]
[Organization]

## Template 2: Safety Incident

**Use when**: A physical safety incident has occurred involving staff, students, or the public.

---

STATEMENT FROM [ORGANIZATION NAME] REGARDING [BRIEF INCIDENT DESCRIPTION]

[Date]

[Organization name] is aware of [factual description of incident — what occurred, when, where]. Our first priority is the safety and wellbeing of [affected group].

[If injuries occurred]: We can confirm that [number] individuals were [description of injuries/status]. We are in contact with their families and are providing [support offered].

[Actions taken]: We have [concrete steps — e.g., "secured the area," "cooperated fully with [Agency Name]," "launched an internal review"]. [Ongoing response actions.]

We are committed to a full and transparent investigation. We will share additional information as it becomes available. Our next update will be issued by [date/time].

Media inquiries: [Name, Title, Email, Phone]

## Template 3: Leadership Departure (Unplanned)

**Use when**: A senior leader's departure is unexpected, involuntary, or involves circumstances that require careful handling.

---

[Date]

[Organization name] announces that [Name] has [departed / stepped down from / left] the organization as of [date].

[If circumstances allow]: [One sentence of context, approved by legal.]

[Interim leadership]: [Name, Title] will serve as [interim role] effective immediately.

[Forward-looking statement]: The organization remains committed to [mission/ongoing work]. [One sentence on continuity.]

We wish [Name] well in [his/her/their] future endeavors.

For questions: [Contact name, email, phone]

---

## Template 4: Public Controversy or Criticism

**Use when**: The organization is subject to public criticism, viral social media, or media scrutiny over a decision or incident.

---

STATEMENT FROM [ORGANIZATION NAME]

[Date]

We are aware of [description of the criticism or controversy — be factual, not defensive].

[If criticism is valid]: We take this feedback seriously. [Specific, concrete action being taken. Not vague: "We are listening" is insufficient.]

[If criticism involves a misunderstanding]: We want to clarify [factual correction, supported by evidence]. [Do not be dismissive. Acknowledge why the concern arose.]

[Accountability]: [If an error was made, name it directly. "We made a mistake" builds more trust than passive language.]

[Path forward]: [What happens next. Specific commitments, not vague reassurances.]

We are committed to [relevant organizational value]. Questions may be directed to [Name, Title, Email].

---

## Template 5: Program or Service Discontinuation

**Use when**: A program, product, or service is being ended and stakeholders will be affected.

---

[Date]

[Organization name] has made the difficult decision to [discontinue / end / sunset] [program/service name], effective [date].

**Why**: [Honest, direct explanation of the reasons — financial, strategic, capacity. Do not obscure.] This was not a decision we made lightly.

**What this means for you**: [Specific impact on affected stakeholders. What they lose access to and when.]

**Transition support**: [What the organization is providing to help people transition — referrals, data exports, grace periods, alternative resources.]

**Questions**: Contact [Name] at [email] by [date] for assistance.

We are grateful for [specific acknowledgment of the community/people affected].

[Name, Title]
