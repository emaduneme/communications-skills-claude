# voice-samples.md — Reference File

## What This Is
3–4 professional emails you've sent that you're happy with. The skill uses these to match your natural email voice — how you open, close, and structure messages — rather than generating something that sounds generic.

## What To Add
Paste real emails you've sent across different contexts. Aim for variety:
- One to a senior person (chair, dean, supervisor)
- One to a peer or collaborator
- One to a student or mentee
- One to an external contact (optional)

Remove any sensitive details (names, organizations) if needed — just use placeholders like [Colleague] or [University].

## Why Variety Matters
Your voice shifts slightly depending on who you're writing to. Providing examples across contexts lets the skill calibrate not just your baseline voice but also how you adjust it.

## Example Structure
```
--- EMAIL 1: To department chair, requesting meeting ---
Subject: Quick question about spring schedule

Hi [Chair's name],

I wanted to check in about the spring course assignments before they're finalized.
Would you have 15 minutes this week to talk through a scheduling conflict?

Thanks,
[Name]

---

--- EMAIL 2: To collaborator, research update ---
[paste email here]

---
```
