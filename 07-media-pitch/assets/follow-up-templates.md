# Follow-Up Email Templates

Use these templates for following up on unanswered pitches. Wait at least 5 business days before sending a first follow-up. Maximum two follow-ups total — then move on.

---

## Rules for Follow-Ups

- Keep it under 3 sentences
- Add a new element when possible (updated data, a new news hook, a relevant development)
- Never express frustration or guilt-trip non-response
- If timing has changed significantly (the story broke elsewhere, the news cycle moved on), acknowledge it or don't follow up
- One follow-up per pitch is ideal; two is the absolute maximum

---

## Template 1: First Follow-Up — No New Development

**Timing**: 5–7 business days after original pitch

---

Subject: Re: [Original subject line]

Hi [First name],

Wanted to briefly follow up on my note from [day/date] about [1-sentence reminder of the story]. Happy to provide additional data, a draft, or arrange an interview at your convenience.

[Name]
[Phone]

---

## Template 2: First Follow-Up — With New Development

**Timing**: 5–7 business days after original pitch, or sooner if a timely development makes it relevant

---

Subject: Re: [Original subject line] — [New angle or development in 3-5 words]

Hi [First name],

Quick follow-up on [topic] — [one sentence describing the new development that adds urgency or a fresh angle]. The original finding I mentioned [one sentence connecting to the pitch].

Let me know if this is a fit. Happy to talk any time this week.

[Name]
[Phone]

---

## Template 3: Second (Final) Follow-Up

**Timing**: 5–7 business days after first follow-up. This is the last one.

---

Subject: Re: [Original subject line]

Hi [First name],

Last note on this — totally understand if the timing isn't right. If it becomes relevant to a future piece, I'm glad to be a resource on [topic/beat].

[Name]
[Phone]

---

## Template 4: Follow-Up After a Related Story Runs

**Use when**: The journalist published a story on a related topic and your research is still relevant

---

Subject: Your [piece title] — related research on [topic]

Hi [First name],

Saw your piece on [topic] — [one genuine, specific observation about the story]. The research I mentioned earlier connects directly to [specific element they covered]: [one sentence of the finding and its relevance].

Would this be useful context for a follow-up or related piece?

[Name]
[Phone]

---

## Template 5: Op-Ed Follow-Up

**Use when**: Following up on an op-ed submission or pitch

---

Subject: Re: Op-ed submission — [Title or topic]

Hi [First name],

Following up on the op-ed I submitted on [date] about [topic in 5 words]. Happy to revise the angle or trim the length if helpful. The piece is under exclusive consideration with [outlet] until [date], at which point I'll be free to place it elsewhere.

[Name]
[Phone]

---

## Template 6: Podcast Booking Follow-Up

---

Subject: Re: Guest pitch — [Your name]

Hi [First name],

Quick follow-up on my note about [topic]. I'm still happy to discuss [specific talking points you offered] — and I've since [new development: published a piece, given a talk, had a relevant experience worth mentioning].

Let me know if this might be a fit for an upcoming episode.

[Name]
[Phone / website / previous appearance links]

---

## Notes on Timing

| Pitch Type | First Follow-Up | Second Follow-Up | Give Up |
|-----------|----------------|-----------------|---------|
| News story pitch | Day 5–7 | Day 12–14 | Day 15+ |
| Op-ed submission | Day 7–10 | Day 14–21 | Day 22+ |
| Expert source pitch | Day 5–7 | Day 12–14 | Day 15+ |
| Podcast booking | Day 7–10 | Day 21–28 | Day 29+ |

Podcast and column editors work on longer timelines than daily journalists. Adjust expectations accordingly.
