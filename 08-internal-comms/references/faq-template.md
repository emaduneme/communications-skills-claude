# faq-template.md — Change-Specific FAQ Builder

## What This Is
A template for building a FAQ document to accompany internal announcements about significant changes. The skill uses a completed FAQ to ensure the announcement covers what employees will actually ask — and to generate the FAQ document itself.

## What To Add
For each major change you're communicating, fill in the FAQ below. Be honest about what you don't know yet — that's better than false certainty. The skill can help you draft answers once you've listed the questions.

## How To Use
Either fill this out before running the skill, or share it during the conversation. The skill will ask you to work through the questions together if you haven't filled it in yet.

## Core Questions

**1. What exactly is changing?**

[Answer — be specific. Vague answers here cause more anxiety than the change itself.]

**2. Why is this change happening?**

[Answer — be honest about the real reasons, acknowledging any constraints on what you can share.]

**3. When does this take effect?**

[Answer — include key dates and transition timeline.]

**4. How does this affect me specifically?**

[Answer — address the most common role-specific concerns. If some people are unaffected, say so explicitly.]

**5. What stays the same?**

[Answer — anchor people with what isn't changing.]

**6. What happens if I have concerns or questions?**

[Answer — name a specific person, channel, and response time. Not "reach out to HR."]

**7. Will there be an opportunity to give input or feedback?**

[Answer — be honest. If the decision is final, say so. If there's still flexibility, explain how feedback will be considered.]
